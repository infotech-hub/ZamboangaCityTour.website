  • 1st Destination
    Fort Pilar and Museum

  • Address
    W32M+F2R, Street, Zamboanga City, Zamboanga del Sur

  • History
    Fort Pilar, crafted by De Vera as his magnum opus, underwent abandonment in 1663 only to be meticulously reconstructed in 1718. Throughout centuries, this bastion stood resiliently, acting as a formidable defense structure against the relentless onslaughts of Moros, Dutch, Portuguese, and British forces. Following a period of neglect and decay subsequent to World War II, the National Museum of the Philippines undertook the task of restoring Fort Pilar to its erstwhile grandeur, ensuring the preservation of its historical significance and architectural magnificence.
  • 2nd Destination
    Santa Cruz Island

  • Address
    Santa Cruz Bank, Basilan Strait, Zamboanga City, Zamboanga del Sur

  • History
    Great Santa Cruz Island, nestled in Zamboanga City, Philippines, is renowned for its unique pink coralline sand, situated 4 kilometers south of the city center. This distinct hue results from finely pulverized red organ pipe coral, shaped by millennia of surf erosion, blending seamlessly with pristine white sand. Since the 1970s, it has attracted international visitors, gaining popularity as one of the world's rare Pink Sand Beaches, the only one in Asia. National Geographic recognized it in 2017 among the esteemed 21 Best Beaches in the World.

Destination Highlights

1st Destination

Fort Pilar , a historic fortress in Zamboanga City dating back to the 17th century, reflects a blend of Spanish and Muslim influences. Originally built for defense against pirates, it now symbolizes Zamboanga's rich cultural history and houses a shrine dedicated to Our Lady of the Pillar. The Zamboanga City Museum preserves the region's diverse history through exhibits featuring artifacts and traditional costumes. As an educational hub, the museum offers insights into Zamboanga's multicultural heritage.

2nd Destination

Santa Cruz Island in Zamboanga City, Philippines, is famous for its rare pink sand beach, created by crushed red organ-pipe corals mixed with white sand. Part of the Great Santa Cruz Islands, its unique pink hue makes it a picturesque destination. With crystal-clear turquoise waters, the island is ideal for swimming and snorkeling.

3rd Destination

Kayawan Beach in Zamboanga City, Philippines, is a pristine coastal gem with soft white sands and clear turquoise waters. This idyllic destination offers a tranquil retreat for beach lovers. Enjoy the sun sea, and serene surroundings at Kayawan Beach, perfect for relaxation and natural beauty enthusiasts in Zamboanga City.
